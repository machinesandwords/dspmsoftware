Cyera vs. Varonis

Cyera and Varonis are the most frequently co-evaluated DSPM pair for enterprise security teams, and they represent the clearest articulation of the primary architectural fault line in the market: agentless cloud-native classification vs. behavioral analytics on data access. The surface-level overlap is real — both find sensitive data, both surface risk, both integrate with remediation workflows. The architectural difference is fundamental: Cyera was designed to scan what data exists and classify it at cloud scale; Varonis was designed to watch who accesses data and detect anomalous behavior. Those are different answers to different questions.

Cyera wins when
  • The data estate is primarily cloud-native — S3, Snowflake, Databricks, cloud databases, SaaS exports — with limited on-premises footprint
  • Shadow data discovery is a named requirement: finding data in locations outside formal governance is the primary use case
  • Agentless deployment speed matters: no capacity for collector infrastructure management, or the environment changes too fast for agent-based scanning to keep up
  • AI-native classification depth is the priority: the environment includes complex unstructured data that pattern matching handles poorly
  • The threat model is data exposure and misconfiguration, not insider threat or compromised credential scenarios
Varonis wins when
  • The data estate is hybrid or predominantly on-premises: file servers, SharePoint, NAS, on-premises databases are significant coverage requirements
  • Real-time behavioral detection is a primary requirement: the threat model includes ransomware staging, insider threat, or compromised credentials acting on data
  • Automated least-privilege remediation is a named requirement: the team cannot manually review every overprivileged identity finding
  • Financial services, healthcare, or legal environments with active compliance requirements for access audit trails and behavioral monitoring
  • The security team needs to detect what is happening to data in real-time, not just what the data posture is at scan time
The real decision

Cyera and Varonis answer different questions. Cyera answers: what sensitive data exists, where is it, is it exposed, and is there data we don't know about? Varonis answers: who is accessing sensitive data, is that behavior normal, and what do we do when it isn't? If your primary question is the first one, Cyera is the more purpose-built answer. If your primary question is the second, Varonis is the only platform that answers it with genuine depth.

The environments where this comparison is hardest are organizations with significant both cloud-native and on-premises data risk. In those cases, neither platform is wrong, but each will leave a gap the other fills. Some organizations run both for different coverage layers. If that is the direction, scoping each deployment clearly against the layer it covers — Cyera for cloud discovery and classification, Varonis for on-premises behavioral governance — avoids the overlap becoming redundant rather than complementary.

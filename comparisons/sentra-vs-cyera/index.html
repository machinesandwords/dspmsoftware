Sentra vs. Cyera

Sentra and Cyera are the closest comparison in the cloud-native pure-play tier — both agentless, both fast to deploy, both built around AI-era data exposure. The difference is less architectural and more about company maturity and reference depth. Cyera has scaled faster and deeper into enterprise accounts; Sentra is newer and leaner, with a narrower but sharper focus on cloud-only classification speed.

Ownership & status — verify before shortlisting

Both Cyera and Sentra remain independently held as of mid-2026, with no announced acquisitions. Cyera has raised substantially more capital — its most recent disclosed valuation is $9 billion as of a January 2026 Series F led by Blackstone — and reports roughly $150 million in annual recurring revenue. Sentra's funding and revenue scale are smaller and not publicly disclosed at the same level of detail; this is relevant context for buyers weighing long-term vendor stability and roadmap investment pace.

Sentra wins when
  • The organization is cloud-first with no meaningful on-premises footprint requiring DSPM coverage
  • Time-to-value and a clean, fast deployment matter more than reference depth at extreme enterprise scale
  • Mid-market budget constraints make Cyera's enterprise-oriented pricing a stretch
  • Petabyte-scale efficiency and low compute overhead are explicit evaluation criteria
  • AI pipeline coverage for specific platforms like Copilot and Bedrock is the primary driver
Cyera wins when
  • The data estate includes meaningful on-premises infrastructure alongside cloud and SaaS
  • Enterprise reference depth and a larger funding and revenue base matter for long-term vendor risk assessment
  • Identity-data correlation through partnerships like the Saviynt integration is a near-term requirement
  • The organization wants AWS Marketplace or AWS Security Hub procurement paths
  • Broader AI Guardian coverage across multiple vector database platforms is needed today
The real decision

This is a closer comparison than most in the category, and the honest answer is that both platforms solve the same core problem well for cloud-native organizations. The differentiator is less about features and more about company stage: Cyera has scaled into a $9 billion, well-capitalized enterprise vendor with deep reference accounts, while Sentra remains leaner and is explicitly positioned for organizations that want fast time-to-value without enterprise-scale overhead or pricing.

For large enterprises with mixed cloud and on-premises estates and a need for vendor stability at scale, Cyera is the lower-risk default. For cloud-first, mid-market organizations prioritizing speed and a tighter budget, Sentra is a credible and often preferable alternative, with the caveat that its smaller reference base is worth weighing for any organization where vendor longevity is a primary concern.
